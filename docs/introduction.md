Chakram is a fast, CPU-mineable cryptocurrency rooted in Kerala's ancient Travancore trading legacy. Anyone can mine it — on a laptop, a desktop, a VM, or a spare Android phone plugged in at night. Transactions confirm in 60 seconds. The smallest unit is a Cash. The network lives on every device that chooses to run it. It started with one genesis block containing a Malayalam message, mined by one person who believed a coin could have a soul.
